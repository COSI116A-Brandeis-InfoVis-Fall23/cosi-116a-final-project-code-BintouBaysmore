Project Team #6: MBTA transit accessibility and customer satisfaction, COSI 116A F23

Bintou Baysmore, Pranav Praburam, Gabriella Vukomanovic

Project-long Course Project as part of COSI 116A: Information Visualization, taught by Prof. Dylan Cashman, Brandeis University.

Motivation

Our research explores the different transit systems and make comparisons on cleanliness, speed, and crime rates to ensure that riders have knowledge on how their public transportation experience in different areas will be.

Visualization

Include the interactive visualization as part of this page. Static example follows.

Data Analysis

We are focusing more on the goal of appealing to the users and allowing them to select a specific area and get information about the transits which will decide whether they will use it. Having this information provided will be greatly appreciated and have more people to use public transportation. Data provided will have the amount of people of color in the area throughout the years, the cleanliness of the station, and the crime rate in the area. 3 things most important that'll impact the usage of public transportation.

Design Process

We have provided information on each station rather than each train to avoid cluttering the page with too much information as these visualizations are comparing the stations of each transit in the United States.

Conclusion

We added the map and charts of the data of a specific stop in different transits in America to compare with the MBTA and to have that information available.
